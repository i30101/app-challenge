What is a Pomodoro Timer?

The Pomodoro Technique was created by Francesco Cirillo in the 1980s. It divides work into intervals of 25 study minutes and 5 break minutes. The intervals of study are known as pomodoros from the Italian word for tomato, as Cirillo used a tomato-shaped timer as a college

Benefits of Timed Study

Time Management

For anyone who finds time management difficult, the Pomodoro Technique does all the planning and work division for you.

Better Focus

At one point, everyone has lost their focus after hours of exhausing work. But working for short intervals with breaks in between reduces fatigue, prevents burnout, and retains your concentration.

Small Simple Steps

By splitting tasks into smaller chunks, the Pomodoro Timer makes your tasks seem less difficult. Small 25-minute chunks of work are much easier to conquer than a huge project. It also breaks habits of procrastination and multitasking.
